Fun Facts

Did You Know?

Mercury is so close to the Sun that from its surface, the Sun would appear three times larger than from Earth.

Venus rotates backwards compared to most planets and is so hot that lead would melt on its surface.

Earth is the only known planet with life and liquid water on its surface.

Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system - Olympus Mons - which is three times taller than Mount Everest.

Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a storm larger than Earth and has been raging for at least 300 years.

Saturn is the least dense planet - it would float in water! Its rings are made of billions of ice particles.

Uranus rotates on its side, possibly due to a collision with an Earth-sized object billions of years ago.

Neptune has the fastest winds in the solar system, reaching speeds of 2,100 km/h.
